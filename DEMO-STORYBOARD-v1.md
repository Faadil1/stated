# STATED — Signature Demo Storyboard v1.0

## Target
Primary video: 60–90 seconds  
Extended demo: up to 3 minutes

## Judge memory sentence
STATED preserves the gap between what a builder said before the outcome and what they could show afterward.

## Core visual device
A two-column receipt:
- WHAT WAS STATED
- WHAT WAS SHOWN

The unresolved gap is the visual center, not a generic success badge.

## Sequence

### 0–8s — Problem
Show a polished final project, then scattered commits, screenshots, notes, and links.

Copy:
> What did I promise before I started?

Voiceover:
> After a build, I can show the result. But I can rewrite the story of what I meant to finish.

### 8–20s — State it first
Show the create screen:
- promise
- deadline
- three conditions
- irreversible warning
- wallet confirmation
- transaction confirmation

Voiceover:
> STATED records what “done” means before the outcome exists.

### 20–25s — Build
Fast authentic montage:
- terminal
- tests
- frontend
- deployment

Voiceover:
> Then I build normally.

### 25–38s — Attach once
Show:
- evidence manifest
- two evidence items mapped to three conditions
- canonical hash
- one-time confirmation
- transaction

Voiceover:
> When the build is over, I attach one final evidence manifest.

### 38–55s — Signature receipt

```text
WHAT WAS STATED              WHAT WAS SHOWN

Verified Monad contract  →   Contract transaction
Three working flows      →   Demo video
Public receipt           →   —
```

Then show:
- 1 CONDITION UNACCOUNTED FOR
- ATTACHED ON TIME

Voiceover:
> The receipt does not pretend everything was completed. It preserves what was stated, what was shown, and what is still missing.

### 55–70s — Tamper test
- original manifest: INTEGRITY MATCH
- edit one character
- reload: INTEGRITY MISMATCH
- restore original
- reload: INTEGRITY MATCH

Voiceover:
> Change the evidence later and the receipt stops matching the onchain record.

### 70–80s — Close
Full-screen receipt and STATED wordmark.

Copy:
> WHAT WAS STATED.  
> WHAT WAS SHOWN.

Voiceover:
> STATED. A promise-versus-proof receipt for builders.

## Integrity rules
- no fake transaction states
- no database presented as onchain truth
- no COMPLETED badge
- no hidden missing condition
- no claim that hash verification proves truth
- no staged result presented as live
- no unsupported Monad claims

## Visual direction
Editorial, restrained, receipt-like.

Avoid:
- purple gradients
- glowing Web3 orbs
- excessive pills
- fake terminal chrome
- generic dashboard cards
- chain-link clichés
- entrance animations hiding content

Prioritize:
- typography
- alignment
- visible timestamps
- receipt materiality
- negative space
- one memorable unresolved line
- precise status language
